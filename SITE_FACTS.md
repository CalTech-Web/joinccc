## Services Offered

### 1. Housing Navigation
- Dedicated, comprehensive, and ongoing Case Management for qualified Medi-Cal members
- Primary goal: achieving an approved month-to-month or fixed-term residential lease agreement
- Duration: 12 Months
- Serves members experiencing homelessness or at risk of homelessness
- Coordinated under CalAIM Community Supports
- URL: `/` (listed on homepage under Provider Services)

### 2. Housing Deposit Voucher
- Funds initial move-in costs for qualified members
- Covers Security Deposit and First Month's Rent, up to $5,000
- Once-per-member-per-lifetime benefit (Terms and Conditions Apply)
- Duration: One Time
- URL: `/` (listed on homepage under Provider Services)

### 3. Tenancy Sustaining
- Continued dedicated, comprehensive, and ongoing Case Management after housing is secured
- Primary goal: sustaining the established tenancy for the duration of the term and beyond
- Duration: 12 Months
- URL: `/` (listed on homepage under Provider Services)

### 4. Cash Assistance Vouchers
- Monthly cash assistance for qualifying members
- Eligibility based on Minimum Basic Standard of Adequate Care (MBSAC) household income levels
- Duration: 12 Months
- URL: `/` (listed on homepage under Provider Services)

### 5. Enhanced Care Management (ECM)
- Acts as Personal Healthcare Assistants for qualified members
- Helps members manage the ongoing administrative demands of their healthcare needs
- Members focus on improving health alongside a dedicated friend and advocate
- Duration: 12 Months
- URL: `/` (listed on homepage under Provider Services)

### 6. Day-Habilitation
- In-person coaching by a specialized caregiver for housed members who qualify
- Teaches social and administrative skills necessary for independent living
- Subjects include financial planning, technical assistance, corresponding with government agencies, and more
- Duration: TBD (Coming Soon)
- URL: `/` (listed on homepage under Provider Services)

## Process (How It Works)

| Phase | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Step 1 | Apply | Qualified Medi-Cal member submits an application at joinccc.org/apply-now with name, contact info, IEHP ID, and housing status details. |
| Step 2 | Eligibility Review | CCC reviews the application to confirm the member meets CalAIM Community Supports eligibility criteria, including homelessness risk and chronic condition factors. |
| Step 3 | Case Manager Assignment | A dedicated case manager is assigned to act as the member's personal advocate and coordinate all housing and healthcare services. |
| Step 4 | Housing Navigation | The case manager actively works to secure an approved residential lease for the member over a 12-month engagement. |
| Step 5 | Sustained Support | Once housed, Tenancy Sustaining services continue for up to 12 months to ensure stable long-term residency and independent living. |

## FAQ Content

**Q: Who qualifies for CCC's services?**
Qualifying individuals must be Medi-Cal recipients who are experiencing homelessness, at risk of homelessness, or recently institutionalized. Applicants must also have an IEHP ID number and may be asked about chronic physical or psychological conditions. CCC reviews all applications to confirm CalAIM Community Supports eligibility.

**Q: How do I apply?**
Applications are submitted online at joinccc.org/apply-now. The form requires your name, phone, email, address, IEHP ID number, and answers to a few housing and health status questions. You can also call (888) 582-2282 for assistance.

**Q: What is the Housing Deposit Voucher and how much is it?**
The Housing Deposit Voucher covers initial move-in costs such as security deposit and first month's rent, up to $5,000. It is a once-per-member-per-lifetime benefit, so it can only be used once. Terms and conditions apply.

**Q: How long do services last?**
Most services, including Housing Navigation, Tenancy Sustaining, Cash Assistance Vouchers, and Enhanced Care Management, run for 12-month terms. The Housing Deposit Voucher is a one-time benefit. Day-Habilitation duration is to be determined.

**Q: What is CalAIM and how does it relate to CCC?**
CalAIM (California Advancing and Innovating Medi-Cal) is a statewide initiative by the California Department of Health Care Services that expands Medi-Cal to include Community Supports such as housing navigation and Enhanced Care Management. CCC is an approved provider delivering these services to eligible members.

**Q: What is Enhanced Care Management?**
Enhanced Care Management is a 12-month service where CCC staff act as Personal Healthcare Assistants for members who have difficulty managing their healthcare needs. Your case manager coordinates appointments, paperwork, and services so you can focus on improving your health.

**Q: Are CCC's services free to members?**
CCC's services are funded through Medi-Cal as CalAIM Community Supports. Qualifying members receive services at no direct cost. Contact CCC at (888) 582-2282 or apply@joinccc.org to confirm eligibility.

## Key Messaging / Brand Voice

- CCC is a CalAIM Community Supports provider, not a general social services agency. The specialization in Medi-Cal housing support is the core identity.
- The member is always referred to with warmth and dignity. Case managers are described as "a dedicated friend and advocate," not a bureaucrat or caseworker.
- Results are stated in concrete numbers: 3,000+ active members, 600+ housed, $1.7M+ in vouchers funded.
- Services remove barriers. The messaging emphasizes removing the specific obstacle (deposit, paperwork burden, housing instability) rather than describing abstract help.
- Eligibility is reassuring, not gatekeeping. The tone should make qualifying members feel encouraged to apply, not intimidated.
- Compliance and trust are prominent. References to HIPAA, CCPA, CalAIM, and reCAPTCHA build institutional credibility with both members and referral partners.
- The brand is teal-forward, clean, and professional. Copy should be direct, warm, and free of jargon where possible.
